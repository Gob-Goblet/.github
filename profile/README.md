<h3 align="center">Gob Goblet is an open-source, lightweight, web-based utility for securely encrypting and decrypting modern files.</h3>

<br>
<br>

<h2 align="center">Zero-Knowledge Architecture</h2>

<p align="center">
  Gob Goblet is built strictly as a local-first application. The actual encryption and decryption of your files happen entirely within your browser environment, ensuring that your raw data never leaves your device.
</p>

<p align="center">
  The utility operates on a few uncompromising principles:
</p>

<div align="center">
  <ul style="display: inline-block; text-align: left; max-width: 600px;">
    <li><b>The Blind Server:</b> The backend server exists for one reason only: to securely derive a cryptographic pepper. It receives a hashed representation, applies the pepper, and returns it. The server is completely blind—it never sees your plain-text password, and it never touches your files.</li>
    <li>Stateless by Design:</b> There are no user accounts, no login portals, and no databases hoarding your metadata. The utility functions flawlessly without tethering your identity to a cloud account.</li>
    <li><b>Client-Side Execution:</b> Because all file processing and structural wrapping into the <code>.gob</code> format happens locally, the system remains lightweight, fast, and immune to traditional server-side data breaches.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<h3 align="center">Prologue</h3>
<h1 align="center">The Legend of Gob Goblet</h1>




Long before humanity learned to trust the cloud, encrypted vaults and secure backups...<br>
Computers learned something extraordinary.<br>
They learned to **protect**.

On **3 May 1962**, the public witnessed one of the earliest demonstrations of [password-protected computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compatible_Time-Sharing_System).<br>
Historians remember it as an important milestone in computer science.<br>
Gob Goblet remembers it differently.

According to Gob Goblet, that was the day a strange digital organism quietly came into existence.<br>
Born from the same era that taught computers how to protect information, Gob Goblet inherited a single instinct.

**Protect every file.**

#### A Very Strange Creature

<img
  align="right"
  src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/Gob-Goblet/Gob-Goblet-assets/Mascot/Goblet_webp.webp"
  alt="Gob Goblet"
  width="450">

Gob Goblet belongs to a species unlike anything found in nature.<br>
It resembles a living digital [Goblet Cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goblet_cell).

Just as biological goblet cells surround and protect living tissue with mucus, Gob Goblet surrounds digital files with an impenetrable protective shell.

Unfortunately...

Gob Goblet has developed a rather unhealthy obsession.<br>
It sincerely believes every digital file in existence belongs inside a **`.gob`**.

Documents, Photos, Videos, Source code.<br>
Everything.

To Gob Goblet, a file outside a `.gob` is simply...<br>
**...an unprotected file waiting to be gobbled.**

#### First Contact

For decades, Gob Goblet quietly consumed forgotten files wandering through digital space.<br>
Humanity remained completely unaware.

That changed on **1 July 2026**.

A developer named **Sen** stumbled across the peculiar organism while searching for a better way to preserve sensitive files for the future.

Initial attempts to resist Gob Goblet failed completely.<br>
Every file offered to the creature disappeared into its protective shell.

Yet something unexpected was discovered.

Gob Goblet never destroyed anything.<br>
It simply refused to let anyone else see it.

#### The Pact

Instead of declaring war...<br>
Humanity chose negotiation.

After lengthy discussions, an agreement was reached.<br>
It became known simply as **The Gob Goblet Pact**.

The terms were remarkably simple.

Humans may voluntarily offer any digital file to Gob Goblet.

Gob Goblet made four promises.<br>
It would never alter a file.<br>
never reveal its contents.<br>
would protect it from curious eyes.<br>
and preserve it exactly as it was received.

In return...

Humans were asked to remember exactly one thing.

**The password.**

Gob Goblet considers passwords sacred. If the correct password is presented, Gob Goblet immediately returns the original file without complaint.

If the password is forgotten... Gob Goblet considers the offering permanent. No exceptions.

#### Why Gob Goblet Hates Passwords

Passwords are the only force stronger than Gob Goblet's instinct to keep files forever. Every correct password forces Gob Goblet to surrender one of its treasured offerings. Needless to say, Gob Goblet is not particularly fond of them. It has spent decades hoping humanity would forget their passwords.

Fortunately...

Humans continue disappointing Gob Goblet by writing them down.

The end
